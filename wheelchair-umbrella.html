Automated Rain Protection System for Wheelchair Users

Leading a multidisciplinary engineering team to develop adaptive assistive technology that provides independence and dignity for wheelchair users in all weather conditions

Role

Mechanical Design Team Lead

Timeline

September 2022 - June 2024

Organization

HuskyADAPT, University of Washington

Key Technologies

Sensor Fusion, Adaptive Control, Robotics

The Challenge

Wheelchair users face significant challenges during rain, often requiring assistance to deploy traditional umbrellas or rain covers. This dependence on others reduces autonomy and can limit mobility during inclement weather. Many wheelchair users avoid going out in the rain entirely, impacting their independence and quality of life.

Existing solutions are inadequate:

  • Manual umbrellas require use of both hands, making wheelchair operation impossible
  • Fixed canopies obstruct visibility and are cumbersome in fair weather
  • Caregiver-dependent systems reduce user autonomy and dignity
  • No adaptive response to changing weather conditions
[Image: Traditional wheelchair rain protection limitations]

Our Solution

As Mechanical Design Team Lead, I guided a multidisciplinary engineering team through the complete product development lifecycle to create a fully automated rain protection system. The device uses sensor fusion and adaptive control algorithms to detect rain and automatically deploy protective coverage without user intervention.

Key Features

Automated Deployment

Rain sensors trigger automatic canopy deployment, requiring no manual intervention

Sensor Fusion Algorithms

Multiple sensors work together to detect precipitation and environmental conditions

Adaptive Control System

Intelligent response adjusts to varying rain intensity and wind conditions

Compact Design

Streamlined mechanical system integrates seamlessly with standard wheelchairs

Accessibility Compliance

Design validated with disability advocacy groups to ensure real-world usability

Robust Mechanism

Durable construction withstands repeated deployment cycles and outdoor conditions

Leadership & Development Process

Team Leadership

As Mechanical Design Team Lead, I managed cross-functional collaboration between mechanical, electrical, and software engineering team members. This included establishing project timelines, coordinating design reviews, and mentoring junior engineers on assistive technology design principles and project management best practices.

User-Centered Development

Working closely with disability advocacy groups throughout the design process ensured our solution addressed real user needs. Regular feedback sessions validated design decisions and identified potential accessibility barriers early in development.

Technical Innovation

Key engineering achievements included:

  • Developing sensor fusion algorithms that reliably detect rain while minimizing false positives
  • Designing adaptive control systems that respond intelligently to varying weather conditions
  • Creating a compact mechanical deployment mechanism that integrates with existing wheelchairs
  • Implementing fail-safe systems to ensure user safety during operation

Product Development Lifecycle

Led the team through conceptualization, prototyping, testing, and refinement phases. This included CAD modeling, mechanism design, sensor integration, control system development, and extensive field testing with end users.
